Advances in Swarm Robotics

Paul Thomasius, HTWK Leipzig

02.06.2025

What is Swarm Robotics?

  • Multiple identical robots
  • Inspired by natural swarms (ants, birds, fish)
  • Based on local decision-making

How It Works

  • Robots use shared algorithms
  • React only to local environment
  • Global behavior emerges

Why Use Swarms?

Adaptability: Handles dynamic environments
Decentralization: No single point of failure
Scalability: Add/remove units easily

Biological Inspiration

  • Self-organization in nature
  • Examples: Ant colonies, fish schools
  • Efficiency through simplicity

Technological Advances

  • Machine Learning
  • Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)
  • Symbolic Regression

Micro-Macro Evolution

  • Macro: Equation structure
  • Micro: Fine-tuning parameters
  • Trained on real swarm data

Open Challenges

  • Hardware limitations
  • Robot-to-robot communication
  • Human-swarm interaction

Future Applications

  • Medical: microrobots for drug delivery
  • Rescue missions in hazardous environments
  • Construction and exploration
  • Agriculture and logistics

Key Future Directions

  • Heterogeneity (diverse robot roles)
  • Spontaneous hierarchy formation
  • Dynamic behavior adaptation via AI

Conclusion

  • Swarm robotics = bio-inspired, decentralized, and scalable
  • ML and GNNs improve behavior modeling
  • Future success depends on adaptability, heterogeneity & collaboration

Discussion (3 min)

Possible Questions:

  • How close are we to real-world deployments?
  • What ethical concerns could arise?
  • Do humans show swarm behavior?
